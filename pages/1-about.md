# About
Dr. Vinny Raster is a conservation ecologist and geospatial scientist specializing in the application of remote sensing technologies to wildlife protection and habitat management. With over a decade of experience in satellite imagery analysis and machine learning, Dr. Raster has pioneered innovative approaches to monitoring endangered megafauna populations across Africa and Southeast Asia. His research focuses on the intersection of geospatial analysis, conservation of pachyderms, and data visualization, developing sophisticated tools that translate complex environmental data into actionable insights for conservation practitioners. Dr. Raster has published extensively in peer-reviewed journals and served as a consultant to numerous international conservation organizations and governmental agencies. Beyond the laboratory, he is passionate about making geospatial science accessible to non-technical stakeholders through intuitive visualization design and community engagement.
